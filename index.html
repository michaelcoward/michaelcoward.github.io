Interest in IT

General

My interests within IT are quite broad at this stage as I am only a year out of high-school and am still in the very early stages of my studies within the IT field. Nevertheless, programming is the most interesting to me and was the main factor to draw me in to the field. Being able to think creatively and create unique programs to solve problems and/or bring joy to others is the primary reason for my interest. The potential for skills in IT to facilitate entrepreneurship and it’s need in almost every industry are also strong causes for my interest in IT.

Causes of my interest

The YouTube video ‘What Most Schools Don’t Teach’ posted by Code.org and a co-worker who would tell me about his Computer Science program both contributed largely to sparking my interest in IT. I had no prior IT experience upon enrolling and beginning my studies at RMIT.

Choosing RMIT

I chose RMIT as my University of choice because it was the only University in my area that offered Biotechnology as a program which is what I was initially enrolled in. After taking a gap year, my interests and ideals shifted so I sought a transfer in to a IT related program. Paired with the convenience of already being considered a current student at RMIT, and RMIT’s good reputation for its Computer Science and Information Systems programs, I was encouraged to stick with RMIT.

Expectations of my future learning

I expect to learn how to create basic programs from the ground up to act and behave in ways that I intend. In addition, I expect to learn and discover in a more specific sense what interests me within IT and how I can pursue that as a career further down the line. I also assume I will receive a concrete foundation of knowledge to apply to other areas not covered during my studies to assist further learning if I so choose.
