My Background

Current Education

Interest in IT

General

My interests within IT are quite broad at this stage as I am only a year out of high-school and am still in the very early stages of my studies within the IT field. Nevertheless, programming is the most interesting to me and was the main factor to draw me in to the field. Being able to think creatively and create unique programs to solve problems and/or bring joy to others is the primary reason for my interest. The potential for skills in IT to facilitate entrepreneurship and it’s need in almost every industry are also strong causes for my interest in IT.

Causes of my interest

The YouTube video ‘What Most Schools Don’t Teach’ posted by Code.org and a co-worker who would tell me about his Computer Science program both contributed largely to sparking my interest in IT. I had no prior IT experience upon enrolling and beginning my studies at RMIT.

Choosing RMIT

I chose RMIT as my University of choice because it was the only University in my area that offered Biotechnology as a program which is what I was initially enrolled in. After taking a gap year, my interests and ideals shifted so I sought a transfer in to a IT related program. Paired with the convenience of already being considered a current student at RMIT, and RMIT’s good reputation for its Computer Science and Information Systems programs, I was encouraged to stick with RMIT.

Expectations of my future learning

I expect to learn how to create basic programs from the ground up to act and behave in ways that I intend. In addition, I expect to learn and discover in a more specific sense what interests me within IT and how I can pursue that as a career further down the line. I also assume I will receive a concrete foundation of knowledge to apply to other areas not covered during my studies to assist further learning if I so choose.



My ideal job

DISCLAIMER:

At this stage of my studies, I don’t have a clear idea of what avenue of IT I would like to go down and as such, the reasons for my ideal job choice are not heavily dictated by the actual tasks I will be carrying out daily in this position (as unintuitive as that sounds). Normally a natural interest/enjoyment of the tasks required of me in a given position would be of high priority however, in this case, my choice is more-so dictated by the supplementary factors that can influence whether one becomes interested in a job or not.

Position

The ‘Senior Java Developer’ position posted by ‘justdigitalpeople.’ is a role that will involve working as a java developer to produce quality code for them and a partnered SaaS business. It is an ideal job for me because it’s very high paying in which they are offering up to $150K + superannuation. A high paying position is important to me for more than just the obvious perks of consumption; a high salary can also enable me to inch closer to financial independence or retirement. This job is attractive because you can work from home, granting some flexibility in hours and being able to work with independence and a sense of freedom. The final aspect of this position that I find appealing, is that they only want two developers to work with a Development Manager, which means I would be working in a small, personal team where my individual contributions make an impact instead of being a tiny cog in a large team, in a massive corporation.

Skills, qualifications, experience required

The position requires being a senior Java developer and have expertise in a stack of different technology which include java, Spring, Microservices, REST API, MySQL and PostgreSQL. The role also requires an all-rounder capable of learning new technologies quickly.

Skills, qualifications, experience I possess

As a young student, I have very little skills, qualifications or experience in the IT field. I most definitely lack the necessities of the ideal job I chose. I have an extremely basic understanding of some introductory Java programming concepts, a Victorian Certificate of Education and no industry experience whatsoever.

Plan to achieve the skills, qualifications and experience required

I will first complete my tertiary education graduating with a Bachelor of Technology (Computing studies) or similar IT-related program if I choose to change. Then I will set my sights on a junior Java programming role of some kind to gain experience and build upon my skills. I could either continue with my current position if it offers a lot of opportunity for growth or look elsewhere for new more challenging positions that ideally can provide a way to learn the various technologies in the tech stack. Along the way I will if necessary complete online courses or self-teach with the help of the internet on how to become proficient at using the various technologies in the required tech stack that I have not yet learned through my previous positions.
